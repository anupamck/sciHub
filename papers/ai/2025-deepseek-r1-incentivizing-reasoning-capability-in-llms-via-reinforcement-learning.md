## Why I saved this

- Landmark paper showing reasoning can emerge from pure RL without supervised fine-tuning on reasoning traces.
- Introduces a practical pipeline (cold start + multi-stage RL) that rivals OpenAI-o1.
- Shows that reasoning capabilities can be distilled into much smaller models.

## Key points

- Main claims:
  - LLM reasoning can be incentivized through pure RL, without human-labeled reasoning trajectories.
  - DeepSeek-R1 achieves performance comparable to OpenAI-o1-1217 on math, code, and STEM reasoning tasks.
  - Reasoning patterns from large models can be distilled into smaller models (1.5B to 70B) effectively.
- Evidence:
  - DeepSeek-R1-Zero (pure RL, no SFT) develops self-verification, reflection, and dynamic strategy adaptation on its own.
  - An "aha moment" was observed where the model spontaneously learned to re-evaluate and correct its reasoning mid-chain.
  - Distilled models across 6 sizes (1.5B, 7B, 8B, 14B, 32B, 70B) on Qwen and Llama architectures retain strong reasoning.
- Limitations or caveats:
  - DeepSeek-R1-Zero suffers from poor readability and language mixing in its chain-of-thought outputs.
  - For smaller models, distillation outperforms applying RL directly — smaller models lack the capacity to develop reasoning from scratch via RL.

## Methodology

- **DeepSeek-R1-Zero**: Pure RL approach using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) applied directly to the base model — no supervised fine-tuning step. Reward signals come from verifiable tasks (math correctness, code passing tests).
- **DeepSeek-R1**: Multi-stage pipeline — (1) cold-start data to seed readable reasoning traces, (2) RL training, (3) rejection sampling to curate high-quality reasoning data, (4) further SFT + RL to refine.
- **Distillation**: Reasoning data generated by DeepSeek-R1 is used to fine-tune smaller dense models (Qwen2.5 and Llama-3 based, from 1.5B to 70B parameters).

## Notes

- The "aha moment" is a striking example of emergent behavior — the model learns to say things like "Wait, let me reconsider..." without being trained to do so.
- Key insight: RL works for reasoning when you have verifiable reward signals (math proofs, code tests), but may struggle in domains where correctness is hard to verify.
- For smaller models, distillation >> RL. This suggests reasoning emergence requires sufficient model capacity.
